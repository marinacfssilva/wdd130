Sao Paulo

Sao Paulo is the most populous city in Brazil and one of the largest in the world, with more than 12 million inhabitants. It is located in the State that bears the same name, which is located in the Southeast region of the country. The city of Sao Paulo is the country's main economic and financial center. Due to its intense economic activity and large population, the city is characterized by numerous residential and commercial buildings, which dominate the local landscape. The city is located within the Atlantic Forest biome, but much of the native vegetation was deforested in the urbanization process. The Tiete and Pinheiros rivers are the two main watercourses that flow through the city, however both had their course intensely modified to meet the demands of the urbanization process and currently both are highly polluted. Sao Paulo is considered a major cultural center in Brazil, due to the wide variety of museums, concerts, festivals and music shows and gastronomic variety.
